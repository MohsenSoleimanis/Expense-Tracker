# Screener & Recruiting Plan — Generative Interviews
**Phase 1 (Discover) · Step 3 · Segment: Everyday individuals**
_Method grounding: Tomer Sharon (*Validating Product Ideas*) on screeners; Erika Hall (*Just Enough Research*) and NN/g (Maria Rosala, "How to Write Screener Questions") on recruiting; Guest, Bunce & Johnson (2006) on sample size. The golden rule: **recruit for behavioral diversity, not demographics**, and screen OUT people who will waste the session._

---

## 1. Who we're looking for (recruitment criteria)

**Base qualifier (everyone must pass):** an "everyday individual" (general consumer, not a finance/business pro) who has **tried to track or manage their personal spending in the last 12 months** — with *any* method (app, spreadsheet, notes, or in their head). We recruit people with *recent, relevant experience* (Portigal's rule), not the general public.

**Recruit for a spread across these BEHAVIORAL profiles** (this matters far more than age/gender). Target ~8 interviews, budget up to 12 for saturation:

| Profile | Why they matter | Target # |
|---|---|---|
| **Lapsed trackers** — started a system in the last year, then stopped | HIGHEST priority — they hold the churn story we couldn't get from mining | 2–3 |
| **Current active trackers** — using an app/spreadsheet now | The retention story: what makes it stick | 2 |
| **Serial switchers** — tried 2+ apps | The switching forces (feeds the JTBD interview) | 1–2 |
| **Manual / spreadsheet / mental-only** — avoid apps or bank-linking on purpose | Sizes the privacy/manual sub-segment (open Q from Step 2) | 1–2 |
| **Avoiders** — anxious about money, tend NOT to look | Tests the ostrich-effect insight directly | 1 |

> Demographics: aim only for a *reasonable* spread of age (25–55), income band, and gender so you're not designing for one narrow slice. Do **not** set rigid demographic quotas — behavior is the axis that matters.

---

## 2. Screen-OUT criteria (protect the sample)

Reject anyone who:
- Works (or has worked) in **personal finance, banking, fintech, accounting, UX/product, or market research/journalism** — insiders give theory, not lived behavior, and bias the data.
- Has **participated in any user-research study in the last 6 months** — filters out "professional participants" who perform for incentives.
- **Cannot recall a specific recent instance** of the behavior in screening (see Q7 trap below) — vague answerers stay vague for 60 minutes.
- Is a **close friend or family member** — social-desirability bias makes them tell you what you want to hear. Second-degree (friend-of-a-friend) is acceptable with caution.

---

## 3. The screener questionnaire

_Use as a short form (Google Form / Typeform) or a 5-minute phone screen. Mostly **behavioral** questions (what they did) not **attitudinal** (what they think) — behavior predicts fit; opinions don't. Pass/fail logic in brackets._

1. **Which best describes your work?** [open text or list] → *screen out finance/fintech/banking/accounting/UX/product/market-research/finance-journalism.*
2. **In the last 12 months, have you tried to keep track of your personal spending in any way?** (Yes / No) → *No = screen out.*
3. **How did you track it?** (select all: a budgeting/expense app · spreadsheet · notes app/paper · in my head · bank app · other) → *used to route into a behavioral profile; "in my head / bank app only" still qualifies.*
4. **Are you doing that today, or have you stopped?** (Doing it now / Stopped / On-and-off) → *routes into active vs lapsed profile; ensure the sample has both.*
5. **How many different budgeting/expense apps have you tried, ever?** (0 / 1 / 2 / 3+) → *2+ flags a "serial switcher" for the JTBD interview.*
6. **Have you ever connected an app to your bank account, or do you avoid that?** (Have connected / Avoid on purpose / Never thought about it) → *"Avoid on purpose" flags the privacy/manual profile.*
7. **[TRAP — recall test] Think about the last time you logged or checked an expense. Tell me in one or two sentences what happened.** [open text] → *screen out anyone who can't produce a concrete, specific answer. This is the single best filter for a good participant.*
8. **Have you participated in any research interview or focus group in the last 6 months?** (Yes / No) → *Yes = screen out.*
9. **Age range / income band / gender** [optional, for spread only — never a hard quota].
10. **Availability + consent to a recorded 45–60 min video call** (Yes / No).

## 4. Where to recruit (channels, ranked, with bias notes)

| Channel | Pro | Bias to watch |
|---|---|---|
| **Research panels** — User Interviews, Respondent.io, Askable | Fast, screener built-in, avoids *your* network's bias | Slightly skews people comfortable being interviewed; the incentive + screen-out for pro-participants handles most of it |
| **Second-degree network** (ask contacts for *their* contacts) | Cheap, high trust, quick | Social-desirability bias; never interview close friends/family |
| **Local / community** (Nextdoor, local FB groups, community centers, library boards) | Best for demographic + income breadth, reaches non-techy "everyday" people | Slower; verify they meet the behavioral criteria |
| ❌ **Avoid as primary:** r/personalfinance, Hacker News, fintech Twitter/X | — | These skew *highly engaged / techy* — the opposite of "everyday individuals." Fine for the *secondary* PullPush mining, wrong for primary recruits. |

## 5. Incentives, logistics, ethics

- **Honorarium:** ~US$75 for a 60-minute session (NN/g norm for general consumers is ~$50–125; pay fairly — it improves show-rate and honesty). Give it regardless of what they say.
- **Format:** remote video (Zoom/Meet) is standard and fine for this; record **video + audio** with permission. 45–60 min.
- **Over-recruit by ~20%** to absorb no-shows (recruit 10 to run 8).
- **Informed consent (mandatory for financial topics)** — before recording, confirm: purpose, that it's recorded, how data is stored and anonymized, that they can skip any question or stop anytime, and that they should **not read out account numbers or full card numbers**. Get a verbal "yes" on the recording.
- **Scheduling buffer:** 15 min between sessions to write up immediate impressions (memory decays fast).

---

## 6. Timeline (realistic)
- Week 1: finalize screener, post to channels, begin screening.
- Week 2: schedule + run first 4–6 interviews.
- Week 3: run remaining interviews **to saturation** (stop when 2 consecutive interviews surface nothing new — Portigal's rule, ~Guest et al.'s ~12 ceiling).
- Rolling: analyze as you go (don't wait for all 12) so you can adjust the guide.
