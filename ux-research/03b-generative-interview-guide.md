# Generative Interview Guide — Everyday Individuals & Money
**Phase 1 (Discover) · Step 3 · ~60 minutes, semi-structured**
_Method grounding: Steve Portigal (*Interviewing Users*, 2nd ed.) — story-based, non-leading, follow-the-participant interviewing; Indi Young (*Practical Empathy*) on deep listening; Erika Hall (*Just Enough Research*). This is a **guide, not a script** — a checklist of what to cover, in the participant's order, not yours._

---

## Facilitation rules (re-read before every session)
These separate a professional interview from a chat:
1. **Ask for stories, not opinions.** "Tell me about the last time you…" beats "Do you like…". Past behavior is data; predicted behavior is fiction.
2. **Never lead.** No "Wouldn't it be easier if…", no "Do you wish it had…". You'll get polite agreement, not truth.
3. **Never pitch your idea or ask them to design.** You're here to learn their world, not test your app. If they ask what you're building, deflect warmly ("I'll share at the end — today's about you").
4. **Embrace silence.** After they answer, wait 3–5 seconds. They'll fill it with the real answer.
5. **Follow their energy.** If they light up about something off-guide, go there. Come back later.
6. **Probe with neutral prompts:** "Tell me more about that." · "What happened next?" · "What was that like?" · "Why did you do it that way?" · echo their last words as a question.
7. **Get specific.** "You said it got tedious — walk me through a specific day that felt tedious."
8. **Watch for the gap** between what they *say* and what they *do* — ask to see their actual app/spreadsheet if remote-screen-share is possible ("grand tour").

## SECTION 0 — Warm-up & consent (0–5 min)
- Thank them; set expectations: *"There are no right or wrong answers — you're the expert on your own life. I'll mostly ask you to tell me stories about things you've actually done."*
- **Consent:** confirm recording, anonymization, right to skip/stop; remind them **not to read out full account or card numbers**. Get the verbal "yes."
- Easy opener: *"To start, tell me a bit about yourself — what does a normal week look like for you?"*

## SECTION 1 — Their relationship with money (5–15 min)
_Goal: emotional context + the ostrich signal. (Research Q3: what role does anxiety/trust play?)_
- *"How would you describe your relationship with money, in general?"* → probe feelings, not systems.
- *"Tell me about the last time you felt good about your money. What was happening?"*
- *"Tell me about the last time money stressed you out."* → probe: *"When that happens, what do you do?"* (listen for **avoidance / not looking** — the ostrich effect).
- *"When you think about checking your bank balance right now — what's that feeling?"*

## SECTION 2 — Grand tour of what they do TODAY (15–27 min)
_Goal: actual current behavior, in detail. (Research Q4: where is the friction, really?)_
- *"Walk me through how you keep track of your spending right now — start to finish."* (If possible: *"Can you share your screen and show me?"*)
- *"When did you last add or check something? Take me back to that exact moment — where were you, what were you doing?"*
- *"What's the most annoying part of doing this?"* → then: *"And the part that feels worth it?"*
- *"Is there a moment in your day or week when you usually do this? What triggers it?"* (habit anchor — feeds retention design.)

## SECTION 3 — The abandonment story (27–40 min) ⭐ highest-value
_Goal: the churn TIMELINE we could NOT get from review mining. (Open Q1.)_
- *"Tell me about a time you started tracking your spending and then stopped."*
- *"Take me back — what made you start in the first place? What was going on in your life then?"*
- *"Walk me through how it wound down. Was there a specific moment you stopped, or did it fade?"* → probe hard for **when** and **why** (was it tedium? forgetting? it worked so you didn't need it? life event?).
- *"After you stopped — did anything change? Did you miss it?"*
- ⭐ Listen carefully for **graduation vs failure**: *"Did you stop because it stopped being useful, or because it got too hard, or something else?"*

## SECTION 4 — Awareness vs. effort (40–48 min)
_Goal: test the reframed thesis — does logging create awareness, and is the effort worth it? (Reframe from Step 2 Reddit data.)_
- *"When you log or write down a purchase, does it change anything about how you feel or what you do next? Tell me about a time it did."*
- *"Some people say entering expenses by hand makes them more aware of spending; others find it a chore. Where do you land — and can you give me an example?"* (balanced framing, not leading.)
- *"Have you ever used something that tracked spending automatically? How did that feel compared to doing it yourself?"* (listen for the **numbness** signal.)

## SECTION 5 — Trust & bank-linking (48–55 min)
_Goal: separate linking-distrust from billing/data-distrust. (Open Q2.)_
- *"Some apps ask to connect directly to your bank. How do you feel about that?"* → probe: *"Have you ever said no to that? What was the reason?"*
- *"Have you ever felt let down or burned by a money app — billing, a subscription, losing data? Tell me what happened."*
- *"What would an app have to do for you to trust it with this?"*

## SECTION 6 — Aspirations & wrap (55–60 min)
- *"If a magic wand made managing your money effortless, what would be different?"* (aspiration, not feature-request.)
- *"Is there anything I should have asked you about but didn't?"* (Portigal's best closing question — often yields the gold.)
- Thank them; deliver the honorarium; ask if you may follow up.

---

## Immediately after each session (do NOT skip)
Spend 10 minutes writing: top 3 surprises, best verbatim quotes, which behavioral profile they were, and any new question to add to the guide. Analyze rolling — adjust the guide as themes emerge (this is why we recruit to saturation, not a fixed N).

## Analysis (after ~6–8)
- Transcribe; do **thematic analysis** (Braun & Clarke) — same method as the review mining, so findings stack.
- Build **affinity clusters**, then draft **personas + a journey map + JTBD statements** (that becomes Phase 2: Define).
