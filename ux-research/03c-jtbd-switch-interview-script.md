# JTBD "Switch" Interview Script — Why People Adopt or Abandon a Money Tool
**Phase 1 (Discover) · Step 3 · ~45–60 minutes**
_Method grounding: Bob Moesta & Chris Spiek (ReWired Group) "Switch" interview; Clayton Christensen (*Competing Against Luck*) Jobs-to-be-Done & the **Four Forces of Progress**. Purpose: reconstruct the causal timeline of a real switch so you learn WHY change happened — the question generative interviews and surveys can't fully answer._

> Run this with **serial switchers** and **lapsed trackers** (from the screener). Use it *instead of* or *after* the generative guide, not both crammed into one hour.

---

## The core idea
People don't "buy an app" — they **"hire"** something to make progress in their life, and to do it they must **"fire"** whatever they used before (even if that was *nothing / doing it in their head*). Your job: find the struggling moment that started the switch, and the forces that pushed and pulled them through it.

### The Four Forces (map every interview onto these)
```
        PUSH of the situation  ──►     ◄── HABIT of the present
   (what wasn't working, the pain)      (inertia, comfort of the old way)

        PULL of the new solution ─►     ◄── ANXIETY about the new
   (what attracted them)                 (fear, risk, "what if it's worse")
```
A switch happens only when **Push + Pull > Habit + Anxiety.** Design later attacks Anxiety and Habit — so hunt for them here.

---

## Step 0 — Pick the switch (2 min)
Ask them to choose ONE concrete event, the more recent and specific the better:
- *adopted* their first tracker, **or**
- *switched* from one app/method to another (e.g., Mint → something), **or**
- *quit* a tracker entirely (a "fire without a hire" — very informative).

*"I want to focus on one specific time. Take me back to when you [started / switched to / stopped using] ______. Let's rebuild that story together, like a timeline."*

---

## Step 1 — The timeline (reconstruct backwards from the event) (10–20 min)
Anchor to concrete time, place, and people. The Moesta timeline has stages — walk them all:

1. **First thought:** *"When did the idea of [getting/changing/quitting] first cross your mind — even faintly? What was happening in your life around then?"*
2. **Passive looking:** *"Between that first thought and doing anything about it — what changed? Did you notice other options, hear people mention apps?"*
3. **The triggering event:** *"What was the specific moment that pushed you from thinking about it to actually doing something? A bill? A surprise? A conversation?"* ⭐ This is the **Push** — get it vividly.
4. **Active looking / deciding:** *"Once you were seriously looking — what did you do? What did you compare? What almost stopped you?"*
5. **The decision:** *"Take me to the day you decided. Where were you? What tipped it over?"*
6. **First use:** *"What were the first hours/days like? What surprised you? What made you nervous?"*
7. **Ongoing → did it stick?** *"Fast-forward — is it still part of your life? If it faded, when and why?"* (loops back to the churn timeline.)

**Facilitation:** keep asking *"and what happened right before that?"* to fill gaps. People remember emotions and events, not dates — anchor with *"was it before or after [a holiday, a paycheck, a move]?"*

---

## Step 2 — Force-by-force probes (15–20 min)
Now make each force explicit.

**PUSH (the pain of the old way):**
- *"What specifically wasn't working with how you did it before?"*
- *"Tell me about the last straw — the moment the old way clearly failed you."*

**PULL (the attraction of the new):**
- *"What did you imagine the new app/method would give you?"*
- *"What was the one thing you hoped would be different?"*

**ANXIETY (fears about the new):** ⭐ design ammunition
- *"What worried you about switching? What were you afraid might go wrong?"* (listen for: bank-linking, losing history, cost, learning curve, being judged.)
- *"Was there anything that almost made you NOT do it?"*

**HABIT (pull of the status quo):**
- *"What made the old way hard to give up, even though it wasn't great?"*
- *"How long did you stick with the old way before actually changing?"*

---

## Step 3 — Fire / Hire language (5 min)
- *"When you started using the new thing — what did you stop using? What did you 'fire'?"* (Even "I fired my own memory" or "I fired the spreadsheet" is a finding.)
- *"What job were you really hiring it to do for you?"* → push past features to the **underlying progress**: control? peace of mind? proof to a partner? saving for a goal?
- *"Did it actually do that job? Where did it fall short?"*

---

## Step 4 — Wrap (2–3 min)
- *"Knowing what you know now, would you make the same switch? Why?"*
- *"Anything about that whole experience I didn't ask about?"*
- Thank + honorarium.

---

## How to analyze switch interviews
1. Lay each participant's story on the **timeline** and the **Four-Forces diagram**.
2. Look for **patterns in the triggering events** (the Push) across people — these are your real acquisition moments (e.g., "a surprise overdraft," "moving in with a partner," "Mint shut down"). Marketing and onboarding should meet people *at* those moments.
3. Catalogue **Anxieties** — each one is a trust/onboarding obstacle your design must dissolve (e.g., "afraid to link my bank," "afraid I'll lose my Mint history," "afraid it's too complicated").
4. Name the **Jobs** people hired for — these become the backbone of your value proposition and your Phase-2 JTBD statements:
   *"When ___ (situation), I want to ___ (motivation), so I can ___ (expected outcome)."*
5. Cross-reference with the generative-interview themes and the review-mining themes — where all three agree, you have high-confidence direction into Phase 2 (Define).
