# Survey Instrument — Spending & Tracking Habits
**Phase 1 (Discover) · quantitative complement to the interviews**
_Method grounding: Caroline Jarrett (*Surveys That Work* — the practitioner authority on survey design); NN/g (Susan Farrell, "Open-Ended vs. Closed Questions"); Christian Rohrer's NN/g framework (surveys = **attitudinal + quantitative** → they measure *how many / how much*, never *why*). Pricing section uses the **Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter** (the accepted method for survey-based pricing)._

> **Where this fits:** surveys answer *how common* a thing is; interviews answer *why*. Ideal order is **qual → quant**: run 3–4 interviews first so questions aren't guesses, then launch the survey wide to quantify. We can seed it now from the Step-2 review-mining themes (already evidence-based), then refine after your first interviews.

> **Question hygiene (Jarrett's rules) applied throughout:** one idea per question · no leading wording · avoid asking people to predict future behavior · offer "I don't/N/A" escapes · keep it ~5–7 minutes.

---

## Intro (shown before Q1)
> *This anonymous ~5-minute survey is about how people keep track of their spending. There are no right answers. Please don't enter any account or card numbers. You can skip any question.*
> **Consent:** by continuing you agree your anonymous answers may be used for product research.

---

## Section A — Context (routing)
**A1.** Do you currently keep track of your personal spending in any way?
- ☐ Yes, regularly ☐ Yes, on and off ☐ No, but I used to ☐ No, never
*(If "never" → skip to Section E + demographics.)*

**A2.** Which methods have you used? *(select all)*
- ☐ A budgeting/expense app ☐ A spreadsheet ☐ Notes app / paper ☐ My bank's own app ☐ Just in my head ☐ Other: ___

**A3.** *(if app used)* Which have you tried? *(select all)*
- ☐ Monarch ☐ YNAB ☐ Copilot ☐ Rocket Money ☐ PocketGuard ☐ Monefy ☐ Money Manager ☐ Mint (before it closed) ☐ Other: ___

## Section B — Behavior
**B1.** How often do you record or review your spending?
- ☐ Multiple times a day ☐ Daily ☐ A few times a week ☐ Weekly ☐ Rarely

**B2.** How do you usually record an expense?
- ☐ Automatically (bank sync) ☐ Manually, myself ☐ A mix ☐ I don't record individual expenses

**B3.** How soon after spending do you usually record it?
- ☐ Right at the moment ☐ Same day ☐ Within a few days ☐ I don't record it

## Section C — Awareness vs. effort *(quantifies the reframed thesis)*
_5-point: Strongly disagree → Strongly agree_
**C1.** "Recording my spending myself makes me more aware of where my money goes."
**C2.** "Keeping my spending records updated feels like a chore."
**C3.** "When an app tracks my spending automatically, I pay *less* attention to it."

## Section D — Why people stop *(quantifies churn themes from the mining)*
**D1.** Have you ever started using a spending tracker/app and then stopped? ☐ Yes ☐ No *(→ if No, skip D2)*
**D2.** What was the **main** reason you stopped? *(pick one)*
- ☐ Too tedious to keep up ☐ I kept forgetting ☐ It got too complicated ☐ Bank sync kept breaking ☐ I didn't trust it with my data ☐ It was too expensive ☐ I didn't find it useful ☐ **I no longer needed it — I had my spending under control** ☐ Other: ___
  *(The bolded option captures "graduation" — a key Step-2 finding; watch its share.)*

## Section E — Trust *(separates bank-linking fear from billing/data fear — open question from Step 2)*
**E1.** How comfortable are you linking an app directly to your bank account? *(Very uncomfortable → Very comfortable, 5-pt)*
**E2.** What worries you **most** about a money app? *(pick one)*
- ☐ Being charged / hard to cancel ☐ Losing my data ☐ Privacy of my bank login ☐ It being inaccurate ☐ Nothing really worries me
**E3.** Have you ever been frustrated by a money app's billing or subscription? ☐ Yes ☐ No ☐ Never used a paid one

## Section F — Emotion *(tests the ostrich effect)*
**F1.** *(5-pt agree)* "Sometimes I avoid checking my bank balance because I don't want to see it."

## Section G — Value & price *(optional; Van Westendorp — treat as directional only)*
**G1.** Have you ever paid for a budgeting/expense app? ☐ Yes ☐ No
**G2–G5.** Thinking of an app that tracks your spending well, at what **monthly price** would it be…
- …so expensive you wouldn't consider it? $___
- …expensive, but you'd still consider it? $___
- …a good value? $___
- …so cheap you'd question its quality? $___

## Section H — Open-ended *(max 2 — keep qual light in a survey)*
**H1.** What's the single most frustrating thing about keeping track of your spending?
**H2.** If a money app could do ONE thing perfectly for you, what would it be?

## Section I — About you *(optional, for segmenting)*
- Age range · Country/region · Life situation (student / employed / freelance-self-employed / not working) · Household (live alone / with partner / with family)

## Distribution & sample
- **Target N:** aim for **50–150**. Friends + friends-of-friends + 2–3 relevant online communities. Directional at 50; segment comparisons (e.g., students vs. employed) need ~100+ per group, which you likely won't hit — so report the whole sample, not thin slices.
- **Reach beyond your bubble:** friends share your age/income/tech context. Post in a couple of *general* communities (not r/personalfinance — too expert) to widen it.
- **Anti-spam:** add Cloudflare Turnstile (free) if public.

## Analysis plan (don't just collect)
- **Closed questions:** simple counts + cross-tabs (e.g., D2 reason × E1 comfort). A few charts.
- **Open questions (H1/H2):** thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke) — same coding you used on the review mining, so it stacks.
- **Triangulate:** compare survey prevalence against the mining themes and your friend-interviews. Agreement across all three = high confidence.

> This survey deliberately measures the exact things Step 2 surfaced (churn reasons, awareness-vs-effort, where distrust concentrates, ostrich avoidance) — so it *quantifies* your qualitative findings instead of starting from scratch.
